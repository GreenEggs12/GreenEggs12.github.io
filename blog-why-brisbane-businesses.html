Why do all Brisbane based businesses need
to branch out over the internet?

  • July 28, 2019 /

  • 5 comments /

  • by Samuel Smith

During 114 BCE, the Silk Road trade routes spanned a distance from Rome to China in which a trader would carry jewellery and other goods took 2 years to complete a single trade. Now in 2019, your target demographic can be relaxing indoors with high filtered air conditioning. Which shields them from the terrible summer heat. They're sitting on the couch that was delivered on an Ikea truck during the first week that they moved into their apartment, watching the new season of Stranger Things premiering on Netflix streaming from thein new smart tv from Kogan.com, delivered the previous week. All the while browsing their phone looking through YOUR businesses products and services which you could deliver to their doorstep over the next 2-3 business days via Australia Post.

Why aren't you taking advantage of the 24-hour, 7days a week exposure you could be gathering for your products and services?

Seriously. Why?

Over the span of 2018 1 million+ people in australia joined the pool of active social media users. So if your business isn't yet on the internet you're only giving your customers the chance to view your products during your allotted business hours. Other local businesses on the internet are taking advantage of these hours, advertising overnight on Facebook and Instagram while customers are browsing the internet after finishing work. Driving customers to their websites in which the customer can purchase the products that the local business then ships out the next available working hour.

What does this mean for my business?

This means you'll have more traffic, more sales and ultimately more business opportunities to satisfy a new found and growing customer base.

How do I get my business on the internet?

If your business hasn't stepped into the world of the internet yet the best way to start would be to create a social media account on each big-ticket social media site. These websites consist of:

  • Facebook
  • Instagram
  • Google

If you're selling products online be it digital or physical I'd recommend creating an online store. You can do that by using one of these sites:

  • SquareUp
  • SquareSpace
  • Shopify
To choose a site to host your store on, compare all of the payment plans against each other and choose the right store for your business.


What's next?

Once you've created your online store and are taking in the benefits of your new-found areas of profit for your business, you might like to expand on your mark over the internet.

To do this you could expand the areas of your website, adding new information and products to your store. Or you could hire a Web Developer and a Content Manager to take care of all this work for you while you handle all the other areas of your successful business.


What can a Web Developer do for me?

A Web Developer can create and tailor your website or app to your exact needs 100% of the way. This will help both your branding and customer service interactions. With techniques like chatbots, email marketing, website layout, search engine optimisation and much more. They can even build applications to help automate your internal business processes, making your business more efficient! The possibilities of technology are endless.
